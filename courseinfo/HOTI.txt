<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Intermediate Hands-On Training for Chemical, Biological, Radiological, Nuclear and Explosive (CBRNE) Incidents course provides participants with hands-on practical exercises that reinforce their ability to perform triage and decontamination procedures and classify residual contamination using various monitoring equipment. As part of the one-day course, participants also perform mass-casualty, decontamination, and monitoring operations within a toxic-agent training facility while wearing personal protective equipment (PPE) Level C. The HOT-I course reinforces the skills that are necessary for a safe and effective response to a CBRNE incident.</p>
<p>Below are some, but not all, of the critical skill sets learned during this training program:</p>
<ul>
<li>Use PPE in a CBRNE environment.</li>
<li>Operate sampling and monitoring equipment to detect both radiological and chemical contaminates.</li>
<li>Conduct mass casualty triage operations while wearing PPE.</li>
<li>Conduct mass decontamination and technical decontamination operations.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Prerequisites and Requirements</h2>
<p>To be eligible for the course, the student must successfully complete the following courses:</p>
<ul>
<li>AWR-160, WMD/Terrorism Awareness for Emergency Responders, and</li>
<li>CDP courses that provide CBRNE response training, such as PER-267 Emergency Medical Operations for CBRNE Incidents.</li>
<li>Participants should be able to don and doff PPE Level C, don and doff self-contained breathing apparatus, lift 40 pounds or more, and meet CDP medical criteria.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Course Length</h2>
