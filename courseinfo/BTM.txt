<h1><center>Bomb Threat Management Planning (BTM)</center></h1>

<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Bomb Threat Management (BTM) Planning course introduces students to the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) risk-management process and the development of the BTM plan. The one-day course includes activities that allow responders to apply specific portions of the risk-management process and their newly acquired knowledge about bomb-threat management procedures to critique mock BTM plans. The course culminates in an experiential learning activity in which learners revise a BTM plan using the findings of the individual module learning activities and the knowledge gained throughout the course.</p>
<p>Below are some, but not all, of the critical skill sets learned during this training program:</p>

<ul>
    <li>Correctly identify the key concepts from the prerequisite courses.</li>
    <li>Apply the DHS risk-management process.</li>
    <li>Describe the roles and responsibilities of each member of the bomb threat management (BTM) team.</li>
	<li>Determine appropriate recommendations for improving a mock plan. </li>
	<li>Evaluate and revise a BTM plan for a given venue.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Prerequisites and Requirements</h2>
<p>To be eligible for the course, the student must successfully complete the following course:</p>

<ul>
    <li>IS-100.b, Introduction to the Incident Command System, IS-100, or any of the available ICS-100 series</li>
</ul>

<p>This courses is recommended but is not a requirement:</p>

<ul>
<li>Response to Suspicious Behaviors and Items taught by virtual instructor-led training.</li>
</ul>
