<h1> Field Force Command and Planning for Executives (FFCE)</center></h1>

<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>This course provides executive level managers and leaders with skills that enable them to coordinate and direct responders safely in a field force-type incident. The course provides management-level responders with information regarding disorderly behavior and issues surrounding civil actions. This course develops response managers who understand the appropriate planning actions to respond safely and appropriately to a civil action incident at the management level.</p>
<p>Below are some, but not all, of the critical skill sets learned during this training program.</p>
<ul>
    <li>Apply lessons learned to current and future incidents involving civil actions/disorders</li>
    <li>Develop comprehensive strategic and tactical plans for their organization when preparing for special events, civil disorders, and public assemblies</li>
    <li>Describe the tactics protest groups use during a civil action/disorder event that require a law enforcement response</li>
	<li>Associate and apply legal principles and applicable case and statutory law to their positions, as well as their departmentsâ€™ mission in planning and executing the management of civil actions or disorders</li>
	<li>Apply available force multiplier options based on the purposes and circumstances surrounding their deployment</li>
	<li>Describe the equipment that could be used during a civil action/disorder to provide body and respiratory protection from chemicals</li>
</ul>

<li>IS-100, Introduction to the Incident Command System</li>
<li>IS-200, ICS for Single Resources and Initial Action Incidents</li>

</ul>
