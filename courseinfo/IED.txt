<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Improvised Explosive Device (IED) Search Procedures Course is a one-day course that introduces the student to basic, low-risk search protocols. It provides learning activities that allow students to perform IED searches of a facility, an area, and a route. Students will be assessed through a performance-based assessment on the searches and a multiple-choice test.</p>
<p>Below are some, but not all, of the critical skill sets learned during this training program:</p>
<ul>
<li>Using a bomb-threat management plan, describe the components of the search section.</li>
<li>Describe the general procedures to conduct a safe and legal IED search, based on identified priorities.</li>
<li>Describe the search procedures for different location scenarios.</li>
<li>Working in pairs and using a Room Search Card and Search kit, execute facility, area, and route searches.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Prerequisites and Requirements</h2>
<p>Below are recommended prerequisites, but are not required before attendance:</p>
<ul>
<li>AWR-333: Improvised Explosive Device Construction and Classification;</li>
<li>AWR-337: Improvised Explosive Device Explosive Effects Mitigation; and</li>
<li>AWR-338: Homemade Explosive and Precursor Awareness.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Course Length</h2>
<p>1.0 days</p>
