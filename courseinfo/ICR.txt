<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>This hands-on training course is executed in a round-robin rotation in mass casualty response, decontamination procedures, scene survey and safety, and monitoring. Participants review, discuss information, and practice skills necessary to effectively respond to a CBRNE incident. Below are some, but not all, of the critical skill sets learned during this training program:</p>
<ul>
<li>Principles of Mass Casualty Response,</li>
<li>Decontamination,</li>
<li>Scene Survey and Safety, and</li>
<li>CBRN Monitoring and PPE Level C.</li>
</ul>
<p>At the conclusion of this course, participants will be able to explain the mechanics of a CBRNE response, including triage, decontamination, scene survey, and monitoring operations.</p>
<h2>Prerequisites and Requirements</h2>
<p>To be eligible for the course, the student must successfully complete the following course, or an equivalent awareness training program:</p>
<ul>
<li>AWR-160, WMD/Terrorism Awareness for Emergency Responders</li>
<li>Participants should be able to Don and doff PPE Level C, lift 40 pounds or more, and meet the medical criteria as established by OSHA</li>
</ul>
<h2>Course Length</h2>
<p>1.0 days</p>
